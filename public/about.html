Visual Debts Analytics

This work presents a Visual Analytics approach using multiple interactive coordinate multidimensional views to monitor different aspects of up to 12 types of Technical Debts in software repositories.
These aspects are:

    General architecture of debt items in the project;

    Debt evolution over the versions;

    >Qualitative aspects of the project;

    Relationship between specific debt groups;

    Low-dimensional representation of complex data provided by the repository;

    Employee sentiment analysis regarding the quality and evolution of debts;

About Views

Sunburst

A powerful way to demonstrate part to entire relationships. Allow a quick understanding of deeply nested systems. This can be useful for understanding group distributions and hierarchical systems, presented as data in a tree-like structure.

Multidimensional Projection (t-SNE)

Visual analysis of multidimensional data relies on reducing the dimensionality of the data to visually encode it. Multidimensional projections are among the most important visualization techniques in this context, transforming such large and complex (multidimensional) data sets into scatter plots where patterns reflect some notion of similarity in the data.

Theme River

ThemeRiver visualizes specific variations over time within the dataset. The “river” flows from left to right over time (X-axis), changing in width to represent changes in the thematic strength of the temporally associated data (Y-axis). The colored “currents” that “flow” within the river narrow or widen to indicate a decrease or increase in the strength of a given topic. And the mouseover presents additional information from these flows.

Parallel Coordinates

Parallel Coordinates allows you to compare the characteristic of the data in a set of numerical variables. To show a set of points in n-dimensional space, a backdrop is drawn consisting of x parallel lines, normally vertical and equally spaced. Each vertical bar represents a variable with its own scale. The values are then plotted as a series of connected lines on each axis (time dimension in this case).
